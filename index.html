🧭 Route Philosophy

UAE comes first while everyone is fresh. Eastern Saudi cities (Hofuf, Riyadh, Buraydah) are transit stops — they’re weekend-trip distance from Doha. Maximum time goes to the far, hard-to-reach jewels: AlUla, Madinah, Taif, Al Baha, Abha & Rijal Almaa. Umrah is performed on Days 16–17, leaving a safe two-stage return to reach Doha on the night of 22 Aug. Every day mixes sightseeing (07:00–18:00) with evening driving (18:00–23:30) while the kids sleep.

Documents, Visas & Border Playbook
For Sri Lankan passport holders with Qatar Residence Permits, driving a Qatar-registered private car

🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia — Multiple-Entry Tourist eVisa

GCC residents (any nationality, incl. Sri Lankan) with a Qatar RP valid 3+ months can apply on visitsaudi.com. It is a 1-year, multiple-entry eVisa, up to 90 days per stay — exactly what this trip needs since you exit to UAE and re-enter Saudi. Passport must be valid 6+ months. Under-18s are applied for by a parent. Approval usually 24–48 h.
Fee + mandatory insurance≈ SAR 380 / person
Family of 5≈ QAR 1,845

🇦🇪 UAE — eVisa for GCC Residents

Sri Lankans resident in Qatar need the UAE e-Visa for GCC residents (apply via the ICP smartservices portal / u.ae before travel — not issued at the land border). 30-day visit visa, extendable once. Qatar RP must have 3+ months validity and your profession/status should match your RP. Apply for all 5 travellers ~2–3 weeks ahead.
Fee (typical)≈ AED 250 / person
Family of 5≈ QAR 1,239

🛂 Border 1 & 4 — Abu Samra ⇄ Salwa (QA ⇄ SA)

Pre-register on MSAR portalVehicle + travellers pre-registration gives you the fast lane. From Feb 2026, vehicle insurance for the return into Qatar is arranged online in advance — on-site sales ended.
Qatar exitPassports + RPs scanned, car exit stamp. Istimara (registration) must be in driver’s name or carry a NOC letter.
Saudi entryeVisa check, fingerprints (first entry), customs x-ray. Buy Saudi TPL car insurance (~SAR 120/week — do it online beforehand to skip the queue).
TimingAllow 1.5–3 h. Cross at dawn on a weekday — avoid Thu/Fri evenings. Toilets, mosque & kiosk available on both sides.

🛂 Border 2 & 3 — Batha ⇄ Ghuwaifat (SA ⇄ AE)

Saudi exit at BathaQuick passport scan + vehicle exit. Fuel up before the border on the Saudi side — it’s ~40% cheaper than UAE.
UAE entry at GhuwaifateVisas verified, biometrics photo, car x-ray. Buy UAE TPL insurance for the car (~AED 150, kiosk at border or online). Expect 45 min–2 h.
Returning westbound (Day 6)UAE exit is fast; Saudi re-entry uses your multiple-entry eVisa — biometrics already on file, so it’s quicker the second time.

🕋 Umrah Guide — Days 16 & 17
Arrive Makkah Day 16 (Thu 20 Aug) evening → Umrah that night when the Haram is coolest and calmest → Jumu'ah at the Kaaba on Day 17 (Fri 21 Aug) before the homeward drive

📍 Miqat — where you enter Ihram

A miqat is the boundary you may not cross toward Makkah without Ihram. Your route from Abha follows the coastal highway (Route 5) north — the correct miqat is Yalamlam (As-Sa’diyyah), ~100 km south of Makkah in Al-Lith governorate, right on your road. It has a large mosque, showers, wudu areas, parking and ihram shops.

Plan: shower (ghusl) at the Abha hotel and wear ihram under travel clothes, or stop ~17:30 at Yalamlam: change, pray 2 raka’at, make niyyah — “Labbayk Allahumma ‘Umrah” — then recite the Talbiyah toward Makkah.

Note: had you gone via Madinah→Makkah directly, the miqat would be Dhul-Hulayfah (Abyar Ali). From Taif side it is Qarn al-Manazil (As-Sayl al-Kabir).

🧺 Ihram Checklist

Step-by-Step Umrah

Ihram & Niyyah at Yalamlamإحرام — Ghusl, 2 rak‘ah, intention for Umrah. Men: two white unstitched cloths. Women: normal modest dress. From now: no perfume, no cutting hair/nails, no arguments. Kids may wear normal clothes if not doing Umrah themselves; children CAN perform Umrah with parents guiding the intention.
Talbiyah on the roadتلبية — “Labbayk Allāhumma labbayk…” repeatedly until you see the Kaaba. Teach the kids the response — it makes the 90-minute drive special.
Enter Masjid al-Haram (right foot, du‘a) — go ~22:30 after resting at the hotel. First sight of the Kaaba: pause, make personal du‘a — it is a moment your children will remember forever.
Tawaf — 7 circuitsطواف — Start at the Black Stone corner (green light marker), Kaaba on your LEFT. Point to the stone saying “Bismillāh, Allāhu Akbar” each pass. Keep Alina on shoulders / stroller on the upper mataf if crowded. Between Yemeni corner & Black Stone: “Rabbanā ātinā fid-dunyā ḥasanah…”.
2 rak‘ah behind Maqam Ibrahim then drink Zamzam — fill the kids’ bottles; it’s chilled and unlimited.
Sa‘i — 7 lengths Safa ⇄ Marwaسعي — Air-conditioned gallery, ~2.5 km total. Strollers allowed on the designated lane. Men jog gently between the green lights. Rest at any point — there is Zamzam along the route.
Halq / Taqsir — Men: shave or trim hair (barbers outside on Ibrahim al-Khalil St, ~SAR 10–20). Women: trim a fingertip length. Umrah complete — Mubarak! Ihram restrictions end.
👧 Umrah with kids: do Tawaf after 22:30 or after Fajr (coolest, calmest); use the upper floors with the stroller if the mataf is packed; agree a meeting point (Gate 1 — King Abdul Aziz Gate) in case of separation; write your phone number on a wristband for each child; carry snacks, and let them drink Zamzam freely. Total Umrah time with children: ~3–4 hours with breaks.

Traveller’s Fiqh Notes

▸ As musafir (traveller) you may shorten Dhuhr/Asr/Isha to 2 rak‘ah and combine Dhuhr+Asr, Maghrib+Isha — perfect for driving evenings.
▸ Every Saudi fuel station has a musalla; majors (SASCO, Naft, ADNOC) have family rooms and clean wudu areas.
▸ Qibla: use the compass in this app’s map or any qibla app offline.
▸ In Madinah, the men can pray in Rawdah (book a slot free on the Nusuk app ~1–3 days ahead); women have separate Rawdah hours.
▸ Friday (Days 3, 10, 17): plan sightseeing around Jumu‘ah ~11:45–13:00.
🚙 Driving, Mountains & Desert

⛰ Mountain Driving (Taif · Al Baha · Abha)

Route 15 rides the Sarawat escarpment at 2,000–2,900 m. Use engine braking (L/2 gear) downhill — never ride the brakes (Al Hada & Rijal Almaa descents are 8–10%). August afternoons bring fog + sudden rain in Asir: fog lights on, 60 km/h, never overtake blind. Baboons on roadsides — windows up, never feed. Fuel up before mountain sections; stations thin out. The Abha→Muhayil descent (Day 17) drops 2,000 m in 30 km — check brakes at the viewpoint pull-off first.

🏜 Desert Driving (Days 1, 6, 8, 17–18)

August highway surface exceeds 60°C — keep tyres at the upper recommended PSI and check cold. Watch for camels at dusk — the deadliest hazard on Saudi highways; scan shoulders continuously after 17:00, use high beams when alone. Sandstorms: pull fully off, hazards ON, lights OFF (so no one follows you off-road). Carry 10 L water + sunshades. Cruise 110–120 km/h max; radars (Saher) are everywhere and fines are automatic.

🛡 Road Safety Rules

▸ Kids ALWAYS belted; Alina (4) in her booster even at night in the car-bed — stop-and-sleep beats loose sleeping.
▸ Driver swap/stop every 2 h; hard stop by 23:30 — no all-nighters.
▸ Saudi speed limits: 120–140 hwy (signed), 50–80 urban. UAE: 110–140 with 20 buffer NOT on all roads — obey signs.
▸ Accident in KSA: call 911/993, don’t move cars, wait for Najm/Muroor report — insurance needs it.
▸ Keep tank ≥ ½ on legs marked ⛽ long-gap.

🧒 Kids Travel System

The trip engine: sightsee 07:00–18:00, drive 18:00–23:30 while kids sleep. Feed dinner at 17:30, pyjamas + teeth before rolling, car-bed flat with their own pillows. Download cartoons offline for the two long daylight drives (Days 6 & 17). Surprise bag every 3rd day (QAR 20 toys). Golden rule: one attraction the kids CHOOSE every day. Hotel pools = the real reward — every 2-night stop has pool time scheduled.

🌡 August Heat Protocol

Gulf coast 41–45°C — outdoor sights before 10:00 or after 17:00 only; midday = museums, malls, caves, hotel pool. The blessed exceptions: Taif 33°C, Al Baha 30°C, Abha 28°C — that’s why the mountain leg sits mid-trip. 3 L water/person/day in the car, electrolyte sachets for kids, hats + SPF50. Never leave anyone in a parked car, even “one minute”.
